Size & Speed

You already know that mobile devices are small. That means you have to think differently about content, navigation, forms and presenting data.

To practice working with the limited size of mobile devices:

  • Start with mobile. Design the mobile interface first, rather than starting with desktop and trying to edit it down.
  • Print out mobile templates and sketch screens in actual size.
  • When writing content, set up your page template to be just 4.4 inches wide and use 15 pt font.
  • Try the Hemingway Editor for help keeping your writing crisp.

Keep in mind also that rendering sites in mobile devices tends to be much slower than on the desktop. Users expect to load a site on a mobile device in under 4 seconds, but the average is 9 seconds. While not quite dialup days, you do need to keep an eye on your page load.

So, prioritize, edit and stay focused. Designers and developers need to optimize images (compress, use SVG), minify HTML and CSS. Try Google PageSpeed Insights for an estimate of the speed to render a page on a mobile device, and suggestions where to look for improvements.
